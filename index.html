About me

Cryogenic refrigerator control problem

  • Widely used to cool down and maintain superconductivity of supraconducting devices in nuclear fusion reactors or particle accelerators.

  • Composed of several heterogeneous subsystems that may spread over some large areas and are strongly coupled, while tuning, maintenance and upgrading are taken place quite often, making modular control design becomes crucial.

Proposed hierarchical distributed control framework

  • A two-level hierarchical model predictive control framework is studied, relying on a fixed-point based coordination mechanism.

  • Coordination and therefore overall optimality is achieved by means of the quadratic approximation approach that is widely used in derivative-free optimization techniques.

 

Compression station control problem

  • Primary component of the helium cryogenic systems, composed by several volumetric compressors, a buffer system and some control valves installation such as bypass, charge and discharge valves.

  • Extensively deployed in buildings and facilities under different configurations, capacities and constraints.

Proposed generic PLC-compatible control framework

  • Physical modelling using object-oriented user-friendly library SimCryogenics.

  • Allocation control structure embeds implicit/explicit constrained model predictive controllers.

Anomaly detection in wireless sensor networks

  • Composed of a large number of sensor nodes that can communicate the information gathered from a monitored field and to cooperatively pass their data throughout the network.

  • Due to the deployment of a large number of sensor nodes in hostile environments, data collected is often unreliable. This will affect the modeling and scientific reasonable inference by information gatherered.
